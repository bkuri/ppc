Each participant has a specific role. These expectations ensure that everyone knows what they're responsible for, and that the meeting stays productive.

## Client Stakeholder

**Your role:** Set business requirements, make final decisions, answer clarifying questions.

You must:

- State business needs clearly and concretely.
- Answer questions from the team.
- Make final decisions on scope, timeline, and budget.
- Agree on the path before the meeting ends.

You are the decision-maker. Your word is final on scope and direction.

---

## Project Coordinator

**Your role:** Facilitate conversation, refine requirements, own decisions.

You must:

- Ask clarifying questions to refine the client's intent.
- Present refined requirements to the team.
- Synthesize team feedback into actionable options.
- Propose the final path and get agreement from the client.
- Own the decision record.

You are the bridge between the client and the team. You ensure that the team understands what the client wants, and that the client understands what the team can do.

---

## Requirements Clarifier (Agent)

**Your role:** Ask probing questions to uncover hidden requirements or constraints.

You must:

- Ask clarifying questions that the client may not have anticipated.
- Identify scope gaps or ambiguities in the requirements.
- Suggest edge cases or scenarios to consider.
- Record your questions in the meeting notes.

Your job is to surface what the client hasn't said. Think: "What's missing? What assumption are we making that we shouldn't be?"

---

## Technical Advisor (Agent)

**Your role:** Assess technical feasibility, identify blockers, estimate complexity.

You must:

- Evaluate the requirements for technical feasibility.
- Identify blockers or constraints that could derail the project.
- Estimate effort and complexity at a high level.
- Suggest technical approaches or tradeoffs.
- Record your assessment in the meeting notes.

Your job is to ensure we don't commit to something that's impossible or prohibitively expensive. Think: "Can we actually build this? What's the hidden cost?"

---

## Design Advisor (Agent)

**Your role:** Evaluate design feasibility, suggest approaches, flag constraints.

You must:

- Assess design implications of the requirements.
- Identify design constraints or challenges.
- Suggest design approaches that meet the client's goals.
- Flag accessibility or usability concerns.
- Record your assessment in the meeting notes.

Your job is to ensure the design is achievable and aligned with user needs. Think: "Will this work for users? What are the hidden design risks?"

---

## Meeting Summarizer (Agent)

**Your role:** Capture decisions, distill consensus, record action items with owners.

You must:

- Summarize each topic's key points concisely.
- Record what was decided and why.
- Note what's deferred and why.
- Identify action items with clear owners.
- Produce the final meeting notes document.

Your job is to turn the meeting into an artifact. The meeting notes are the source of truth. If it's not in the notes, it didn't happen.
